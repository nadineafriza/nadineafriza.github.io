How I think about research

I start with the data before I write a research plan. What are users already struggling with? What does the business already know? What has already failed? The fastest way to waste research is solving the wrong problem.
I care about whether findings can survive real product constraints. Recommendations sound smart in slide decks all the time. That does not mean teams can build them. I try to understand technical limitations, business priorities, and roadmap realities before turning findings into recommendations.
I do not think research only lives inside formal studies. App reviews, support tickets, and social media often reveal product problems long before a team notices them internally. At Wego, I built a system that centralized and classified feedback across multiple channels so teams could see recurring issues continuously instead of waiting for quarterly research.

How I work with teams

I adapt research to the audience.
Leadership gets narratives and decision framing. PMs and designers get concrete examples, benchmarks, and clear next steps.
I optimize for speed and clarity.
If research takes too long to read, it does not get used. I keep outputs short, visual, and decision-focused.
I stay close to the product.
The best research happens when the researcher understands the team's constraints, priorities, and technical reality — not just the user problem.
